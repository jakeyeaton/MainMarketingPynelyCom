Inbound Marketing.

Rather than being interruprive. Inbound marketing is a unique approach focused on attracting customers through valuable content and interactions that are relevant and helpful.











Inbound marketing attracts qualified prospects and builds trust and credibility with your prospects. Instead of bothering prospect of "why" you should buy from your business. You educate them.





When investing in outbound marketing. You're investing in fighting for short term attention. When investing in inbound marketing. You're investing in your potential customers for the long term.





Instead of creating an advertising campaign to grab the attention of potential customers. You're creating content designed to address the problems and needs of your ideal customers.

01) Content Marketing

The first step in creating content is to have an active blog. The reason for this is because a blog is the single best way to attract new visitors to your website. But in order to get found by the correct prospective customers, you much first create educational content that speaks to them and answers their questions.

You also need to create content outside of a blog. Why? Some people don't enjoy reading blogs and might find being presented with a more specific in-depth piece of content more valuable such as an eBook, Whitepaper or Infographic.

02) Social Media Marketing

One way is through email marketing and as this is useful. We take the approach of presenting your content first on social media. Across every platform that makes sense for your brand.

You can think of this approach much like casting a large net in order to catch a couple rare fish (leads) who will then be presented with content via email marketing in the future.

But there is much more to social media marketing than just sharing content. In fact, we would argue the most important part of social media marketing is to engage with your prospects and put a human face on your brand. Your brand is human. Correct? (If not. We found aliens!!).

We research your industry, current customers and where your ideal buyers spend their time. We then invest much of our social media marketing time and energy on a few platforms. For example, if you were in the coffee business we would spend a large amount of our social media marketing time and energy on Instagram, Pinterest, and Facebook.

03) Form + Calls-to-action Optimization

Another part of the inbound puzzle is turning those visitors into leads.

In order for visitors to become leads, they much first complete a form and submit their information. But creating any form isn't always the best idea. Creating forms which are as easy and fast as possible to fill out is extremely important.

The same can be said about calls-to-actions. But what are calls-to-actions? They are buttons or links that encourage your visitors to take actions. For example "Download an eBook" or "Attend a Webinar". If you don't have enough calls-to-action or your calls-to-action aren't enticing enough, you simply won't generate leads.

Knowing how important having optimized forms and calls-to-actions are now you can be certain Pynely will optimize this process for you.

04) Landing Page Optimization

What are landing pages? They are where an offer in the call-to-action is fulfilled, and where the prospect submits information that your sales team can use to begin a conversation with them.

For example, if you go over to our resources section and click on a resource. You will be redirected to a landing page which will have a form. After you complete the form you will then be redirected to a thank you page where you can find more information about what you just filled out, follow us, or view our blog.

Again, we will optimize this process for you.

05) Proper Setup of CRM + Workflows

The first is making sure your CRM is setup correctly. The reason? Keeping all your customers together is one place is important. But knowing how they are moving throughout your pipeline is even more important.

The second step is to connect your CRM to your email marketing software and create workflows (marketing automation). Why? Creating email marketing and lead nurturing tailored to the needs and lifecycle stage of each lead.

The third step is to create email marketing content and workflows to tailor to the needs and lifecycle stage of each lead (aka lead nurturing). So, for example, is a visitor downloaded an ebook on a certain topic form in the past, you might want to send that lead a series of related emails. But if they followed you on Facebook and visited certain pages on your website, you might want to change the messaging and content shared to reflect those different interests.

01) Smart text + Social Monitoring Consulting

But the work doesn't end. The inbound way to all about providing remarkable content to your users, whether they are visitors, leads, or existing customers.

When you take advantage of "Smart Text" you're going to want to provide existing customers with remarkable content tailored to their interests and challenges. Educate them. Help them achieve their own goals, as well as introduce new products and features that might be of interest to them.

When your taking advantage of "Social Monitoring" you're keeping track of the social conversations that matter to you most. You do this by listening out for your customers' questions, comments, likes, and dislikes. Of course, you reach out to them with relevant content.
